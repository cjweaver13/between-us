Getting Started

Three steps to begin

1
Open the app Go to between-us-cm.netlify.app — no account, no password.

3
Write or start a session Use Write to process privately first, or go straight to Session to talk together.
How It Works

Two ways to use it

✎
Write — Private Write freely to process your own thoughts. The AI responds just to you. The other person never sees it. Use this before a session or whenever you need to work something out first.
●
Session — Together A live back-and-forth between both of you. The AI reads the full conversation and responds as a counselor after every message. Works even if you're not online at the same time — messages stay and update every 10 seconds.
The AI Counselor

How it's programmed to respond

●
Completely neutral. It does not take sides. Both perspectives are treated as legitimate, even when they directly conflict.
●
Separates emotion from request. It reflects back what you're actually saying — the feeling underneath — before translating it into language the other person can hear without defensiveness.
●
Identifies spirals gently. If something looks like an RSD trigger or cognitive distortion, it names it — without framing it as an attack or taking a side.
●
Asks one honest question. After every message in a live session, it directs one specific question — by name — to move the conversation forward.
●
No filler. No affirmations. It's direct and treats you both as intelligent adults. You won't get "That's so valid!" — you'll get something actually useful.
●
Short responses. Live session responses are under 200 words. Private check-ins under 250. It doesn't ramble.

Expectations

What this is — and isn't

This is a supplement, not a replacement for your actual therapist. Use it between sessions, not instead of them.
The AI doesn't remember previous conversations on its own — but when you use "End Session," it generates a handoff note that carries forward across sessions.
It can surface patterns and ask honest questions, but it can't make decisions for you or tell you what to do.
If something it says feels off or unfair, say so in the session. It will recalibrate.
It works best when you write honestly — not for the other person to read, but for yourself first.
Privacy

Who can see what

Anything you write in the Write tab is private to you — Collin cannot see it, and it never appears in the shared session. Use it freely to process without filtering yourself.

The Session tab is shared — both of you see everything in that thread. The AI counselor's responses are also visible to both.

Data is stored securely. Only the two of you have access.
Tips

Getting the most out of it

Use Write first when you're activated or mid-spiral — get the raw version out privately, then bring a cleaner version into Session.
Pick your mood chips honestly. They give the counselor useful context before it reads your entry.
In Session, let the counselor finish before responding. Read what it says — it often names something worth sitting with for a moment.
Use End Session when you're done. It generates a summary note the counselor will reference next time, so context builds over time.
You don't have to be in a conflict to use it. Check-ins when things are going well help build the record of what's working too.
